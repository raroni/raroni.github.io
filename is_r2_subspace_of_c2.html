The key to answering this question for me was to recall that a vector space is actually defined by a set SSSand a field F\mathbb{F}F. Usually we refer to the typical real vector space by just Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn and implicitly assume that F=R\mathbb{F} = \mathbb{R}F=R. The important insight here is that whenever you deal with a mix of real and complex vector spaces, you have to be careful about what "scalar" means, i.e. what field is used.
